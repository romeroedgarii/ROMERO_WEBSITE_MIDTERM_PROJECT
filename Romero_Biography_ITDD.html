Childhood Years

1I am Edgar II G. Romero, born on October 15, 1999, in Quezon City, Metro Manila. 2At a young age, I was surrounded by music, creativity, and a family filled with love. 3I come from a big family where my parents, sisters, brother, and I shared countless joyful memories. 4Many of these moments were spent cuddled together on our big bed right in front of the TV, watching movies and musicals. 5Classics like "The Sound of Music," "Sister Act" 1-3, "Home Alone" 1-3, the "Harry Potter" films, and Disney musicals were staples of our bonding time. 6These shows did more than entertain; they helped develop my English skills and sparked my love for storytelling and artistry. 7My dad, often like Captain Von Trapp, would pick up his guitar, and we would all gather around on the floor to sing along as he played songs by The Beatles, Bee Gees, Abba, and Queen. 8The music filled our home with joy and connection. 9Singing together was second nature to us, and every family gathering felt like a mini-concert. 10One unforgettable tradition was my performance of Tina Turner’s “Proud Mary”, which became a staple at family events. 11Despite being an introverted child who preferred solving puzzles or getting lost in shows rather than playing with cousins, my parents encouraged me to share my talents publicly. 12Beyond family gatherings, my cousins and I would often participate in barangay singing and dancing competitions, where we proudly showcased our talents to the local community. 13These events were filled with vibrant energy, excitement, and a sense of accomplishment when our hard work paid off on stage. 14Even as a shy kid, those competitions helped me grow more confident in front of an audience. 15Winning or losing didn’t matter as much as the joy of performing together and representing our family. 16My siblings and I found creative ways to bond, whether through music, storytelling, or simply spending time together drawing and painting. I’ve always had a passion for art and loved creating colorful pieces, which my family always appreciated.
17My sisters often joined me in imaginative activities, while my brother had a knack for playful mischief that kept things lively. 18We didn’t just share moments; we built countless stories filled with laughter and music. 19My parents always emphasized that these creative outlets were: "kasing halaga lang 'yan ng pag aaral, 'nak". 20Even on quiet days, music seemed to linger in our home, like a comforting background melody. 21Those times taught me the value of self-expression, whether through song or brushstroke. 22Looking back, I’m incredibly grateful for a childhood where music, creativity, and family love intertwined so beautifully. 23These experiences remain some of my most cherished memories, shaping who I am today and continuing to inspire my passions.







Teenage Years

1My teenage years were filled with growth, creativity, and unexpected triumphs as I studied at Judge Feliciano Belmonte Sr. High School. 2With the artistry I had nurtured during my childhood, it didn’t take long for my teachers to notice my creative talents. 3They encouraged me to represent the school in various poster-making competitions. 4One of the most memorable was the Nationwide Centennial Stamp Design Making Contest for the Centennial Year celebration of the Philippine National Bank. 5Out of about a hundred talented students, I won as a runner-up, an achievement that still stands as a proud moment for me. 6Beyond visual arts, I explored more opportunities to express myself creatively. 7I became a part of the Campus Journalists, serving as both an editorial cartoonist and an anchor for our school's radio broadcasting team. 8Competing from the district level up to the national level, our team brought home numerous awards, contributing to the pride of our alma mater. 9Despite being naturally introverted and often nervous in front of crowds, I constantly found myself pushing past my fears. 10This realization hit me as I reflected on my high school journey—fear never stopped me from showing up and doing what I loved. 11Singing, which had always been a major part of my life, pulled me toward the school's choir. 12I auditioned as a tenor and made it through, marking the beginning of yet another chapter in my creative journey. 13Joining my first choral competition was nerve-wracking but also incredibly fulfilling.
14Balancing academics, journalism, broadcasting, and choir wasn’t easy, but these experiences shaped who I was becoming. 15High school wasn’t just about grades for me—it was about learning and finding myself through these various creative pursuits. 16I may not have graduated as an honor student, but I left with lessons and experiences that were just as valuable. 17One of the most defining moments of my high school years was winning the Artist of the Year award. 18It was a recognition that validated my hard work, passion, and creativity. 19Receiving that award felt like a celebration of my journey—from a child performing in family events to an artist creating meaningful work for competitions and school activities. 20Looking back, these teenage years taught me that success is not just measured by medals or titles but by how much you grow and push past the limits you once thought you had.







College Years

1After high school, I was excited to move straight to college, but my plans took a detour when our batch became the first-ever to experience Senior High School. 2In 2017, I only had one goal: to enter Polytechnic University of the Philippines (PUP) for Senior High School and continue there for college. 3Thankfully, I got in and pursued the Tech-Voc ICT strand, where I learned programming languages like Visual Basic, C++, C, and Java. 4Although my interest in technology grew, I chose a different path for college and entered PUP’s College of Engineering and Architecture to study Bachelor of Science in Architecture. 5To relieve the stress that architecture brought me, I joined Bagong Himig Serenata, the university's choir. 6Music had always been a refuge for me, and this choir became a sanctuary amid demanding studies. 7At the same time, I got my first job as a McDonald's crew member, but I quickly realized how architecture demanded too much time and energy for me to balance work so I had to leave. 8In 2019, my life took an unfortunate turn as I struggled emotionally and financially, lacking the support I needed from my family. 9Unable to cope, I stopped college during my second year to find work and focus on healing myself. 10I was fortunate to land a job as a Customer Service Representative, assisting international clients with telecommunications services. 11Despite the onset of the pandemic, I kept my job and became the family’s financial backbone when no one else was working. 12After half a year, the management recognized my potential and made me part of the training crew. 13I eventually rose to Senior Representative and then Team Manager, where I led my own team. 14However, after two and a half years, the monotony became unbearable, prompting me to switch to a better-paying role in digital real estate management in Metro Manila.
15In 2022, I made a life-changing decision by joining the prestigious choir Los Cantantes de Manila. 16Being surrounded by incredible singers pushed me to develop my musical skills and opened doors I never imagined possible. 17One of the highlights was touring and competing across Europe, visiting countries like Germany, Austria, Spain, Hungary, Italy, and even Russia. 18I often thought back to watching Whoopi Goldberg inspire a choir in "Sister Act", never realizing that one day I would live a similar dream. 19Traveling the world with Los Cantantes de Manila wasn’t just about singing, it reignited my aspirations and offered me a chance to return to school. 20My choir master, who also serves as the choir director at the University of the East - Manila, encouraged me to pursue education again. 21Now, as a proud UE Warrior studying Bachelor of Science in Information Technology, I’ve come full circle. 22While this path is far from architecture, it offers practical opportunities for my future and represents how resilience and passion can steer life toward new possibilities.
